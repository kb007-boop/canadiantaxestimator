Prince Edward Island Income Tax — FAQ

Common questions about Prince Edward Island's 2026 provincial income tax.

What are PEI's 2026 income tax brackets?

Prince Edward Island uses five provincial tax brackets: 9.8% on the first $32,656; 13.8% on $32,656–$64,313; 16.7% on $64,313–$105,000; 18% on $105,000–$140,000; and 18.5% on income over $140,000. PEI's top provincial rate of 18.5% is among the higher rates in Atlantic Canada. Note that a provincial surtax also applies for higher-income earners.

What is PEI's provincial surtax and how does it work?

Prince Edward Island levies a provincial surtax of 10% on PEI income tax above $12,000. This surtax is applied after calculating basic PEI provincial tax. For example, if your PEI provincial tax is $15,000, you would pay an additional 10% on $3,000 (the amount above $12,000), adding $300 to your provincial tax bill. This surtax effectively raises the real marginal rate for middle and upper-income earners beyond the nominal bracket rates. The calculator uses standard bracket rates; actual liability may be slightly higher due to the surtax.

How does PEI compare to New Brunswick and Nova Scotia for income taxes?

Among Atlantic provinces, PEI has the lowest top combined rate (~51.37%), lower than both New Brunswick (~52.5%) and Nova Scotia (~54%). PEI's BPA of $12,000 is more generous than Nova Scotia's $8,481 but lower than New Brunswick's $12,458. However, PEI's provincial surtax of 10% on PEI tax above $12,000 can erode some of this advantage for middle and high earners.
