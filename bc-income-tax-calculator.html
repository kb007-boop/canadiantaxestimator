BC Income Tax — FAQ

Common questions about British Columbia's 2024 provincial income tax.

What are BC's 2024 income tax brackets?

BC has seven provincial brackets: 5.06% on the first $45,654; 7.70% on $45,654–$91,310; 10.50% on $91,310–$104,835; 12.29% on $104,835–$127,299; 14.70% on $127,299–$172,602; 16.80% on $172,602–$240,716; and 20.50% on income over $240,716.

What is the BC basic personal amount for 2024?

The 2024 BC basic personal amount is $11,981. This non-refundable tax credit reduces BC provincial tax by approximately $606 (5.06% × $11,981), while the federal BPA of $15,705 provides an additional $2,355 reduction in federal tax.

Does BC have a surtax?

No. Unlike Ontario and Prince Edward Island, BC does not have a provincial surtax. The nominal bracket rates are the actual rates applied, making BC's tax calculation more straightforward.

How does BC tax compare to other provinces?

BC's combined top marginal rate of ~53.50% is one of the highest in Canada at the top bracket. However, BC's lower brackets are competitive. For $100,000 of income the effective combined rate is around 29–31%. Compare all provinces →

How valuable is an RRSP contribution in BC?

At a $95,000 income in BC, the combined marginal rate is approximately 40.70% (federal 26% + BC 14.70%). A $10,000 RRSP contribution would save approximately $4,070 in combined taxes — a refund you receive in the spring.
